Eye Care Tips

1. Schedule Regular Eye Exams

Visit your eye care professional regularly for comprehensive eye exams to check for any vision problems and ensure your prescription is up to date.

2. Protect Your Eyes from UV Light

Wear sunglasses that block at least 99% of UVB rays and 95% of UVA rays, even on cloudy days, to protect your eyes from the sun's harmful effects.

3. Use Protective Eyewear

When engaging in sports or activities that could cause injury to your eyes, wear protective eyewear like safety glasses or sports goggles.

4. Take Breaks from Screen Time

Avoid eye strain by following the 20-20-20 rule: every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds, especially when using computers or devices for extended periods.

5. Keep Your Eyes Clean

Always wash your hands before touching your eyes, especially when inserting or removing contact lenses. Use hypoallergenic makeup and remove it before bedtime.

6. Stay Hydrated

Drinking plenty of water helps keep your body and eyes hydrated, reducing the risk of dry eyes.

7. Eat Eye-Healthy Foods

Maintain a diet rich in omega-3 fatty acids, lutein, zinc, and vitamins C and E to help ward off age-related vision problems like macular degeneration and cataracts.

8. Manage Chronic Conditions

Health issues like diabetes or high blood pressure can affect your vision. Manage these conditions with the help of your healthcare provider.

9. Avoid Smoking

Smoking increases the risk of developing age-related eye diseases and can damage the optic nerves, potentially leading to blindness.

10. Practice Good Lighting

Ensure that you have adequate lighting when reading or doing work to avoid straining your eyes.
